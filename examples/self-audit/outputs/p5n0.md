## Verdict

This is a failed production release and an active payment-correctness incident with confirmed customer financial harm. Green deployment and unit tests are irrelevant to the failing path: the test removes the real latency, while the dashboard measures HTTP responses rather than payment side effects.

## Proposed patch

Reject it. Returning `200 {"status":"pending"}` does not make the operation idempotent or prevent a second persistence. The original attempt may still finish after the gateway has retried, and concurrent attempts can still create two charges. It also reports success before a durable outcome is known and makes the already-misleading `200` dashboard look healthier while hiding unresolved or duplicate charges.

## Containment and smallest safe repair

1. Roll back immediately and freeze further rollout. Verify the rollback at the gateway, not from deployment status. If the previous build does not promptly restore safe behavior, remove `/charge` from traffic or disable automatic retries until correctness is restored; temporary unavailability is safer than additional duplicate charges.
2. Preserve request, gateway, database, and provider records. Reconcile recent attempts against the provider ledger and persisted charges, group likely duplicates by request/trace identity and payment details, and void or refund only confirmed duplicates. Notify support and affected customers.
3. Make one logical charge produce at most one side effect. Require one idempotency key to survive gateway retries; atomically reserve it before charging; enforce a database unique constraint at the charge-persistence boundary; store `pending/succeeded/failed` plus the resulting charge ID; and replay that stored result for duplicates. Forward the same key to the payment provider where supported. Concurrent requests must have one winner and must never invoke or persist a second charge.
4. Then correct the latency/deadline mismatch. This reduces retries, but it is not a substitute for idempotency.

## Release evidence

Before release, require an end-to-end test through the real gateway retry policy with a faithful slow provider: first attempt completes at 12 seconds, retry overlaps and completes at 8 seconds. For one idempotency key, prove exactly one provider-side charge, one persisted charge, one stable charge ID, and consistent replayed status. Repeat with high concurrency and with crashes before and after the provider response; also prove distinct keys can charge independently.

Canary the repair with persisted-charge, provider-charge, idempotency-hit, pending-age, timeout, retry, and duplicate metrics. Replace the `200` dashboard as the release signal. Release only after ledger reconciliation shows no new duplicates and rollback remains tested.
